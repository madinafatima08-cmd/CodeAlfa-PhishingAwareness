# Phishing Awareness - CodeAlpha Internship Task 1

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack in which attackers pretend to be trusted organizations or individuals to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data.

## Common Types of Phishing

### 1. Email Phishing

Fake emails that appear to come from legitimate organizations such as banks, social media platforms, or online services.

### 2. SMS Phishing (Smishing)

Fraudulent text messages containing malicious links or requests for personal information.

### 3. Website Phishing

Fake websites designed to look like genuine websites in order to steal login credentials and other sensitive data.

## How to Identify Phishing Attempts

* Check the sender's email address carefully.
* Look for spelling and grammar mistakes.
* Hover over links to verify the actual URL before clicking.
* Be cautious of urgent or threatening messages.
* Never share passwords, OTPs, or personal information through email or messages.

## What to Do If You Receive a Phishing Message

* Do not click on suspicious links.
* Do not download unknown attachments.
* Delete the message immediately.
* Report the phishing attempt if possible.
* Change your password if you accidentally shared any information.

## Conclusion

Phishing attacks are one of the most common cybersecurity threats. By staying alert and following basic security practices, users can protect themselves from online scams and data theft.
